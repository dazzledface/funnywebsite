COMP126: Practical Web Design & Development for Everyone

Course Schedule, Fall 2022

go to syllabus & policies

important notes

  • Please note! This schedule is subject to change based on your needs and interests and other, less predictable events. This page is the only up-to-date version.
  • Readings/viewings/setup tasks should be completed before class on the days on which they're listed.
  • Quizzes will begin at the start of class time and will last 30 minutes, unless you have extended time accommodations.

Th 8/18. Read DuRocher, Introduction & Chapters 1, 2, 4, & 5 (pp.1-42 & 63-95). Set up VSCode or another text editor before class: more details at https://comp126.com/setup.html. Join Piazza page with full name & UNC email at https://piazza.com/unc/fall2022/comp126.

Week 2: Introduction to CSS, Box Model

Th 8/25. Continued. Sakai Quiz 1.

Mo 8/29. Assignment 1 Due, 11:55pm EST.

Tu 8/30. Read DuRocher, Chapters 8 & 9.

Th 9/1. Continued.

Week 4: Flexbox

Tu 9/6. Well-Being Day: No class.

Week 7: HTML Media, Forms, Odds & Ends; Aesthetics, Transitions, Animations

Tu 9/27. Class on Zoom only: https://unc.zoom.us/j/99877521047. A2 Q&A & random helpful demos.

We 9/28. Assignment 2 Due, 11:55pm EST.

Week 8: HTML Forms; CSS Aesthetics, Transitions, Animations

Tu 10/4. Read DuRocher, Chapter 12 (pp. 203-220).

Th 10/6. Continued. Sakai Quiz 4 (on HTML forms & odds & ends, CSS aesthetics).

Week 9: Midterm

Tu 10/11. Midterm Q&A. NOTE: This session will be held on Zoom, but the Zoom will be run from the classroom, so you're welcome to attend remotely at https://unc.zoom.us/j/99877521047 or in person.

Th 10/13. Midterm exam.

The exam will be part multiple choice, part coding. It is designed to take 75 minutes, but I'm giving you two hours to account for upload/organizational issues. It will appear on Sakai at 9am EST on Thursday, October 13, and submissions will close at 6pm EST on Thursday, October 13. You may take the exam at any two-hour period you choose during that time, but please note that teaching staff will only be available in the classroom to take questions or solve problems in real time during our regularly scheduled class time.

You may email us with exam-related questions at other times of the day. However, you may not get an immediate response, and your exam timer will not stop while you're waiting to hear back from us. Therefore, if you want to make sure you have direct access to us while you're taking the exam, I recommend that you take it in the classroom.
